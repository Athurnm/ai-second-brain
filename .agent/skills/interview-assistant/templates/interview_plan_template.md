## 3. Interview Structure (60 minutes)

| Segment | Time | Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A. Warm-up & background** | 5 min | Settle nerves; map career story; probe job tenures/transitions |
| **B. CV deep-dive (claims verification)** | 15 min | Verify key numbers and claims; separate candidate's work from team's |
| **C. Technical / domain knowledge** | 10 min | Depth check on platform mechanics, frameworks, or domain-specific logic |
| **D. Case role-play (live scenario)** | 15 min | Action bias, trade-offs, structured thinking under ambiguity |
| **E. Ways of working & collaboration** | 10 min | Team dynamics, conflict management, communication style |
| **F. Candidate questions & close** | 5 min | Check preparation, curiosity, and close |

*Note: Inform the candidate that the session is recorded for assessment purposes. Use Fathom if remote.*

## 4. Question Bank

### A. Warm-up & Background (5 min)
1. Walk me through your journey from [Uni/First Role] to [Current Role] - what pulled you toward [Target Field] specifically?
2. You have had [N] transitions in your career. Walk me through the context and reasons behind each transition.

### B. CV Deep-Dive — Claims Verification (15 min)
1. You mentioned [Key Claim 1 from CV] in your role at [Company 1]. Walk me through how you achieved this: what was the step-by-step strategy, budget/resources, and creative/technical execution?
2. What was the baseline metric before this improvement, and how was it measured? How did you verify it was your contribution rather than the team's or market growth?
3. Who else worked on this with you? What parts of the execution were specifically yours?

### C. Technical / Domain Knowledge (10 min)
1. [Custom Technical Question 1 based on CV claims/Target Role]
2. [Custom Technical Question 2 based on CV claims/Target Role]
3. Explain a time you had to deal with [Common Technical Challenge in Role] - how did you approach it and what did you learn?

### D. Case Role-Play (15 min) — "The Budget/Resource Crunch"
* **Scenario:** You are leading [Target Role's responsibility] for a key project. Mid-month, three things happen at once: (1) [Role-specific complication 1, e.g. performance drop or bug], (2) [Stakeholder complication, e.g. CEO requests a vanity change], and (3) [Resource complication, e.g. team member leaves or budget is cut]. You have [Constraint] to handle this. What do you do?
* **What to evaluate:**
  * Does the candidate diagnose the issue before acting, but without stalling into paralysis?
  * Do they handle stakeholder requests with structured trade-off options rather than blind compliance?
  * Do they commit to a clear, reasoned decision under ambiguity?
  * **Push-back script:** If the stakeholder rejects their plan and demands compliance, how do they handle the conflict?

### E. Ways of Working (10 min)
1. Tell me about a time you had a direct conflict with a teammate or stakeholder (e.g. designer, developer, manager). How did you handle it and what was the outcome?
2. Tell me about a project or campaign that failed. What went wrong, what were the numbers, and what did you change in future projects?
3. How do you prepare a weekly/monthly progress report? What key metrics go on the first page?

### F. Close (5 min)
1. What do you know about what we are building at [Company], and what specifically excites you about this role?

## 5. Evaluation Rubric

This rubric defines the performance levels (Entry, Junior, Medior, Senior) across the competencies required for this role.

### Core Dimension 1: Action Bias & Problem-Solving
* **Entry:** Freezes or escalates everything; waits to be told what to do when context is missing.
* **Junior:** Forms a hypothesis quickly, runs cheap tests, and commits to a decision with available data; escalates with recommendations, not questions.
* **Medior:** Thrives in ambiguity; consistently presents leadership/clients with 2-3 costed options and recommendations; never lets work stall waiting for "perfect data."
* **Senior:** Proactively defines paths under high ambiguity, drives cross-functional execution velocity, and sets clear decision framework SLAs for the team.

### Core Dimension 2: Quantitative Ownership & Honesty
* **Entry:** Quotes results without baselines or definitions; cannot reconstruct how a number was measured; claims team results as personal.
* **Junior:** Knows metrics and numbers cold (budgets, timelines, baseline volumes); transparent about what was personal work vs. team contribution; admits failures.
* **Medior:** Frames all results in clear commercial terms (revenue, conversion, payback); volunteers caveats or limitations in their data before being asked.
* **Senior:** Designs metric structures and OKRs from scratch; establishes full data hygiene; takes absolute accountability for team business outcomes.

### Core Dimension 3: Communication & Stakeholder Collaboration
* **Entry:** Passive; complies with all requests without explaining trade-offs or gets defensive when challenged.
* **Junior:** Reports outcomes against targets with clear narratives; flags problems early with proposed fixes; pushes back on bad ideas respectfully with data.
* **Medior:** Trusted advisor posture; reframes requests around client/business goals; co-creates plans so stakeholders feel shared ownership.
* **Senior:** Directs high-level stakeholder alignment under conflict; builds organizational trust; influences product and engineering direction fluidly.

### Core Dimension 4: Strategic Thinking & OKR Alignment
* **Entry:** Focuses on outputs (shipping tasks/features) rather than business outcomes. Low strategic awareness.
* **Junior:** Understands strategy and OKRs when explained; coordinates daily backlog/tasks to align with set priorities.
* **Medior:** Evaluates trade-offs dynamically using commercial context and company goals; proactively optimizes tasks toward target conditions.
* **Senior:** Proactively gathers company context; translates long-term strategic goals into execution roadmaps; sets the strategy for the team.

## 7. Red Flags Checklist (During Interview)

* [ ] Cannot produce a single concrete number or metric from their current role.
* [ ] Case study claims fall apart under probing (doesn't know the budget, steps, or actual tools used).
* [ ] Percentage claims on CV have no baseline or measurement definition.
* [ ] No genuine failure story - claims everything always worked perfectly.
* [ ] Answers the case by stalling ("I would wait for perfect data") without committing to an action.
* [ ] Complies with all vanity requests without presenting trade-offs or cost-of-delay.
* [ ] Platform/domain knowledge is purely theoretical (definitions) rather than scar-tissue (war stories).
